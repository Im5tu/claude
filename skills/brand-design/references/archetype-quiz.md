# Archetype Quiz

> **Purpose:** Phase 2 of the brand-design skill. Determines which of the 12 Jungian archetypes best fits the brand using 7 fixed core questions plus 2-3 dynamic follow-ups generated from Discovery context. Read by the skill during the Archetype Quiz phase.

## Archetype Weighting for SaaS

Not all archetypes appear equally in SaaS. Weight your scoring accordingly:

- **HIGH** (common in SaaS): Sage, Hero, Creator, Magician, Ruler
- **MEDIUM** (viable): Explorer, Everyman, Caregiver
- **LOW** (rare — require strong signal): Innocent, Outlaw, Lover, Jester

## Core Quiz Questions (Fixed — Ask All 7)

Present each question via `AskUserQuestion`. The user picks A, B, C, or D.

**Question 1:**
"Your product succeeds because it:
A) Empowers users to achieve more
B) Simplifies complexity
C) Transforms how things are done
D) Provides expert guidance"

**Question 2:**
"Your ideal customer relationship is:
A) Trusted advisor
B) Empowering coach
C) Reliable partner
D) Innovative guide"

**Question 3:**
"When a competitor launches a similar feature, you:
A) Focus on doing it better (quality)
B) Do it differently (innovation)
C) Emphasize your community/ecosystem
D) Show your track record"

**Question 4:**
"Your brand should feel:
A) Bold and confident
B) Warm and approachable
C) Smart and precise
D) Fresh and unconventional"

**Question 5:**
"Customer success looks like:
A) Mastery and expertise
B) Freedom and independence
C) Safety and reliability
D) Creative breakthrough"

**Question 6:**
"Your marketing tone is closest to:
A) Stripe — confident, technical, premium
B) Notion — clean, versatile, enabling
C) Mailchimp — friendly, warm, accessible
D) Figma — creative, collaborative, modern"

**Question 7:**
"When things go wrong, your brand:
A) Takes ownership with transparency
B) Provides systematic resolution
C) Rallies community support
D) Innovates past the problem"

## Dynamic Follow-Up Questions (2-3)

After the 7 core questions, generate 2-3 additional questions tailored to:
- Ambiguities or tensions in the Discovery answers
- The emerging archetype pattern from the core quiz
- Industry-specific archetype considerations

These follow-ups help break ties and add confidence to the archetype assignment.

## Scoring and Assignment

Map each answer across all 12 archetypes. Each answer can contribute partial weight to multiple archetypes. After all questions:

1. Rank all 12 archetypes by weighted score.
2. **Clear winner** (>20% lead over second place): Present that archetype with a rationale.
3. **Split result** (top 2 within 20%): Present the top 2 archetypes to the user. For each, give: name, core motivation, and a one-sentence summary of how the brand would feel under that archetype. The user MUST commit to one. Do not proceed with a blend.

## Result Presentation

Present the chosen archetype with:
- Archetype name and core motivation
- How it maps to the user's product and audience
- What it implies for visual identity (high level)
