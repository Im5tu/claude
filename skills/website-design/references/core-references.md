# Reference Websites — Premium Design Exemplars

This file documents websites that represent the ceiling of quality for each style direction. Study these before building anything. The goal is not to copy them — it is to understand the decisions that make them feel earned rather than assembled.

---

## What Makes a Reference Site Premium

Premium sites share a quality that is easier to feel than to name: every element appears to be where it is for a reason. There is no visual padding, no filler section, no stock-photo hero because the hero needed a background. The sections present are there because the business has specific, non-generic content for them. The sections that are absent are absent because the business didn't earn them.

The second distinguishing quality is intentionality in restraint. Template sites try to include everything — they fear the user will miss something. Premium sites make the opposite bet: they trust the design to direct attention, they trust the copy to do the work, and they trust the user to read. The result is that every section feels chosen, not appended. A logo strip on a template site exists because "all sites have logo strips." A logo strip on a premium site exists because those specific logos are genuinely credible and the business knows it.

The third quality is system coherence. Fonts, spacing, color, motion, and copy all feel like they came from the same mind. Not from the same template — from the same point of view. A warm, artisan-inflected ceramics studio and a cold, precise SaaS product both have system coherence, but they have completely different systems. What they share is that nothing breaks the system. No default blue link color in an otherwise monochrome layout. No default HTML button in an otherwise custom interactive section. The discipline of system maintenance is what separates sites that look designed from sites that look assembled.

## Core Reference Sites

These are the foundational exemplars that informed this skill's design principles. They are cited throughout the preset and animation files.

- **adviceguidefp.com** — Financial planning firm with an editorial-grade web presence that punches far above the typical financial services category. Key techniques: heavily constrained color palette (near-monochrome with single copper accent), editorial-weight typography with a serif display font at large scale, long-scroll narrative structure that educates before it converts, hero that leads with a question rather than a claim. Preset affinity: refined-professional with editorial-minimal leanings. Borrow: the question-as-headline device (reframes the visitor's problem rather than stating the firm's solution); the copper-on-neutral accent application as sparse punctuation rather than repeated color.

- **superlist.com** — Productivity SaaS whose marketing site achieves the rare feat of feeling premium without feeling cold. Key techniques: warm off-white background (not pure white) that softens the technical product, product UI shown in context rather than floating on a pedestal, generous whitespace that signals confidence, copy written in plain language without jargon. Preset affinity: clean-saas. Borrow: the warm-neutral background choice that distinguishes from the sea of pure-white SaaS sites; product UI shown at natural scale in realistic context rather than artificially enlarged.

- **slite.com** — Team knowledge base SaaS with marketing site design that feels genuinely editorial. Key techniques: oversized display typography for section headlines that creates visual anchors, generous column margins, alternating text-left / text-right layouts at large scale, deliberate animation restraint (almost nothing moves on scroll — the typography does all the work). Preset affinity: editorial-minimal. Borrow: the decision to let oversized type be the visual weight without needing imagery or motion; column margin usage that creates breathing room without padding inflation.

- **hm.la** — Creative studio or agency with motion-forward identity. Key techniques: fast, snappy entrance animations (not the slow float that most sites use — these feel immediate and intentional), custom cursor interaction, text that moves at the moment it should rather than when a scroll threshold is hit, section transitions that feel choreographed not triggered. Preset affinity: bold-studio. Borrow: animation timing philosophy — fast entrances (0.4–0.5s), tight stagger (60–80ms), modest y-travel (20px not 40px); the feeling that motion is purposeful not decorative.

- **proctorsgroup.com** — Professional services firm with a site that reads as completely un-template-like despite using conventional section types. Key techniques: custom photography with strong directional lighting (not stock), section-level type hierarchy that makes every section feel distinct, footer designed as a genuine destination rather than an afterthought, process section with real specificity rather than generic "Discover → Design → Deliver" steps. Preset affinity: refined-professional. Borrow: the custom photography approach — even with a modest brief, specifying directional lighting and consistent color treatment transforms imagery; footer as a content-rich destination.

- **vouschurch.com** — Faith community site that demonstrates how emotional design works without sentimentality. Key techniques: photography that captures authentic human moments rather than posed congregation shots, warm palette used with restraint, typographic scale that conveys gravity without formality, spatial generosity that communicates welcome without being soft. Preset affinity: warm-artisan. Borrow: the authentic-photography principle — the difference between real human moments and posed content is immediately felt; warmth expressed through spatial generosity rather than through soft fonts or pastel color.

- **june15consulting.com** — Independent consulting site at the extreme of editorial restraint. Key techniques: minimal section count (fewer sections than you think are needed), very high whitespace-to-content ratio, typography doing all the visual heavy lifting, complete absence of stock imagery or decorative elements, every word appears necessary. Preset affinity: editorial-minimal. Borrow: the courage to ship fewer sections with better content — this site could have 10 sections and would be worse; the whitespace-as-design-element approach where empty space is not absence but presence.

- **bravelittlebeast.com** — Creative agency with a site built around kinetic typography and editorial rhythm. Key techniques: all-caps kinetic typography hero (the type itself is the visual, no imagery needed), expanding accordion service selector with smooth reveal animations, asymmetric case study grid with varied card proportions, inline stats rendered as bold numbers without counter animation (the weight of the number is the statement, not the animation), dark theme with textured warm backgrounds that prevent the dark from feeling cold. Preset affinity: bold-studio with dark-luxury motion sensibility. Borrow: the all-caps kinetic hero — when the typography is strong enough, no image is needed; textured dark backgrounds (subtle grain or noise) that add warmth to dark color schemes.

## 2025–2026 Exemplars

These sites represent the current frontier of premium web design in their respective categories.

- **linear.app** — Project management tool whose marketing site set the design vocabulary that a hundred SaaS sites copied. Key techniques: dark theme executed with extraordinary precision (not just a dark background — a system of layered dark surfaces each with precise lightness differences), keyboard-shortcut-as-visual-motif, product UI screenshots that are genuinely beautiful (the product earns its visibility), motion that responds to scroll velocity rather than just position. Preset affinity: dark-luxury. Borrow: the layered dark surface system — linear uses at least 4 distinct dark values that create depth without color; keyboard shortcut badges as both UI and credibility signal.

- **raycast.com** — Developer tool site that achieves warmth in a technical category. Key techniques: gradient backgrounds that use near-neutral warm tones rather than saturated color (warm dark brown-black rather than blue-black), extension gallery that demonstrates product scale, community contributions shown as social proof in a non-conventional format, copy written for developers by people who clearly understand development. Preset affinity: dark-luxury with warm-artisan color sensibility. Borrow: warm-dark color treatment (brown-black or warm-neutral-950 instead of pure black or blue-black) that makes technical products feel approachable; community work shown as genuine social proof.

- **craft.do** — Document editor with aspirational lifestyle marketing. Key techniques: product shown at full fidelity with real content (not "Lorem ipsum" documents), platform-specific screenshots that acknowledge context (Mac, iPhone, iPad each get their own moment), quiet animation that reveals rather than announces, warm off-white surfaces that feel paper-like without being literal. Preset affinity: editorial-minimal with clean-saas structure. Borrow: the real-content-in-screenshots principle — showing a product with real, thoughtful content inside it communicates product quality in ways no headline can.

- **basement.studio** — Creative dev studio that treats the website as portfolio evidence of their craft. Key techniques: custom cursor that changes on hover targets (the cursor becomes part of the brand interaction), grid-based layout with intentional rule breaks at key moments, typographic scale that goes genuinely large (headlines at 20vw+ on desktop), dark mode as default with no light mode (the darkness is intentional not optional). Preset affinity: bold-studio. Borrow: treating hover interactions as brand moments — the cursor change, the card reveal, the link underline animation are each distinct; grid-break at signature moments (break the grid deliberately, not accidentally).

- **resend.com** — Email infrastructure product with a site that makes developer tooling look desirable. Key techniques: code snippet as hero visual (the code IS the product demonstration), dark background with syntax highlighting as accent color source, extremely fast load with no animation ceremony, documentation quality that signals product quality, pricing table designed as a conversion tool rather than a feature comparison grid. Preset affinity: dark-luxury with editorial-minimal restraint. Borrow: using code snippets as visual elements — when the product is technical, showing real code is more honest and more compelling than showing abstract UI; documentation quality as brand signal.

- **loom.com** — Video messaging platform with a site organized around use cases rather than features. Key techniques: section structure driven by audience segments (not feature lists), video content embedded naturally rather than in play-button-on-thumbnail format, social proof organized by business type (startup testimonials separate from enterprise testimonials), conversion path optimized for different stages of purchase readiness. Preset affinity: clean-saas. Borrow: use-case-first structure — organizing sections by who benefits rather than what exists is a fundamental reframe that produces more specific, compelling copy.

- **cron.com** (acquired, archived) — Calendar application whose marketing site became a reference for how SaaS can feel artisanal. Key techniques: product screenshots with extraordinary attention to detail in the product itself, warm-to-cool gradient that implied time of day, extremely spare section count (the site had six sections and felt complete), typeface selection that connected calendar to editorial tradition. Preset affinity: editorial-minimal with clean-saas discipline. Borrow: the six-section completeness principle — this site proved that restraint in section count forces quality of content; product screenshots where the product detail is itself a design element.

- **framer.com** — Design tool with a site built in the product, demonstrating meta-craft. Key techniques: motion that demonstrates product capability (the animations are also the product pitch), component-based page structure that mirrors how designers think, dark hero with light mid-page sections that creates genuine contrast rhythm, interactive elements that invite exploration rather than guiding linearly. Preset affinity: bold-studio. Borrow: motion as product demonstration — when the product produces motion, the site's motion should demonstrate what's possible rather than following generic scroll-reveal patterns.

- **vercel.com** — Developer platform with a site that defines the premium dark technical aesthetic. Key techniques: gradient glow effects anchored by a single accent color (not multi-color gradients), performance metrics shown as design elements (the speed numbers are styled as feature headlines), globe visualization as hero element (abstract but relevant), dark surface system with precisely calibrated border opacity to define card edges. Preset affinity: dark-luxury. Borrow: the single-accent-glow system — using one accent color for gradient halos rather than multi-color prevents the "AI aesthetic" while maintaining visual interest; border opacity as the primary way to define dark-mode card boundaries (not background-color changes).

- **ghost.org** — Publishing platform whose site demonstrates that content-first businesses can have exceptional design. Key techniques: the product is publishing therefore the site itself is published content (the copy reads like editorial writing, not marketing copy), open-source positioning expressed through radical transparency (pricing, revenue, team size all publicly stated), section backgrounds that alternate between warm paper-tone and dark ink-tone creating a print-publishing rhythm, typography that honors the publishing tradition it serves. Preset affinity: editorial-minimal with warm-artisan typography. Borrow: the editorial-writing approach to marketing copy — writing the site as content rather than as sales material produces prose that is more readable and more credible; background rhythm that references the product's context (paper/ink for publishing, terminal/editor-dark for dev tools).

## Applying Reference Sites

When using these references during a build:

1. **Identify the design decision to borrow**, not the aesthetic to copy. "Warm-dark background treatment" is a decision. "Looks like Raycast" is not a design instruction.

2. **Check preset alignment.** A dark-luxury build can borrow from Linear. A warm-artisan build cannot borrow from Linear — the surface system is incompatible with the warmth vocabulary.

3. **Reference the specific technique** in component or section-level decisions. "Ghost.org's background rhythm of warm-paper alternating with dark-ink" is a background-rhythm decision that belongs in the composition phase.

4. **Do not mix reference vocabularies on the same site.** Borrowing cursor behavior from Basement Studio and warm-dark treatment from Raycast and editorial type scale from Slite produces a site that feels like it cannot decide what it is.
